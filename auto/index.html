About Me

In my life journey spanning nearly sixty years, I've dedicated over four decades to military service. Now retired, as I transition from a military career to newfound leisure, I find myself reflecting on the wealth of experiences, lessons, and memories gathered along the way. These four decades have taught me invaluable lessons and I feel compelled to document the defining chapters of my life. Join me to explore the events and experiences of my life. You may find something worthwhile in the simple events. I harbor no grandiose achievements to decorate this narrative. However, in the simplicity of my story lies a reservoir of experiences that, perhaps, may resonate with others navigating the crossroads of life.

Early Life

Born on the 20th of May 1966, in the halls of the Central Government Hospital in Rawalpindi, at 10 PM, my roots lie in a humble family. This humble origin has been the anchor within the storms and contentment of my journey. I am, the youngest in a circle of warmth and companionship. Among the pillars of my world are three elder sisters, Rukhsana, Shahana, and Shakila, as well as an elder brother, Zubair. Their collective love and support have been the cornerstone of my existence. From the earliest days, I have been cocooned in the affectionate embrace of my siblings and parents. As the youngest, I enjoyed the privilege of being the apple of their eyes. I have fond memories of sleeping on my mother's belly, a simple yet cherished recollection of the warmth that defined my early years. My sisters, Rukhsana, Shahana, and Shakila, share tales of sibling rivalry transformed into tender care. They recount spirited battles to be the one to lift me up, to wash my clothes, and to shower me with the nurturing affection that only siblings can provide. Recollections of my early childhood are somewhat hazy, but within the fog of memory emerges a precious image. At the tender age of four, as the morning sun cast its golden hues, my siblings and father get on their daily journeys to school and office. Left behind, I mingled in the quiet moments with my mother. Her melodious rhymes filled the air, creating a sanctuary of love that time could never erase. Those mornings, bathed in the glow of maternal love, remain etched as a cherished chapter of my formative years. In my childhood, a touching note resounds from a chapter when I was around four and a half years old. Life, presented an incident that left an indelible mark on our family narrative. My mother, a vibrant and sociable soul, embarked on a routine visit to our neighbors. However, fate took an unexpected turn at their doorstep. A simple misstep led to a cascade of consequences, as her knee suffered multiple fractures. The repercussions of that moment were profound. She had to remain hospitalized for a long time and after the surgical procedure, doctors replaced her injured knee bone with an artificial counterpart. This period was a test of resilience for our family, especially for my mother, once an embodiment of agility and activity. In the wake of this unforeseen event, my siblings and I, still in the tender embrace of childhood, found ourselves on a journey to our grandmother's (Naani) house in Lahore. For about a month and a half, we became transient residents in grandma's abode, navigating a world where the echoes of our mother's laughter were temporarily replaced by the warmth of our extended family. This event, marked by separation, resilience, and the unwavering support of family, remains etched in the corridors of my memory.
